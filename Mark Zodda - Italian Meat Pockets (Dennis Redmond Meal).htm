Italian Meat Pockets

Dennis Redmond Meal

 

The savory salami-cheese filling in a pocket of tender beef has a very Sicilian heritage, but also a universal appeal. Simple, but unique, this is a wonderful Nick Stellino dish that IÕve made even better by using beef tenderloin and diced tomatoes.

 

Ingredients

1.4 pounds beef tenderloin cut into ½-inch (1.5 cm) slices
8-10 ounces hard salami, good quality 
8 ounces thinly sliced provolone 
Salt

Black pepper
2 Tablespoons olive oil 
1 Tablespoon butter
1/4 cup chopped onion
6 big garlic cloves, thickly sliced 
1 bay leaf 
¼ - ½ teaspoon red pepper flakes
1-14.5 ounce (411 g) can diced tomatoes
1 teaspoon Italian seasoning, without salt
¼ teaspoon oregano
¼ cup dry Marsala
½ cup beef or chicken bouillon 

Directions

Remove any fat or gristle from the beef, then cut slices ½–inch (1.5 cm) thick, cutting across the grain.

Place each slice of meat between 2 sheets of plastic wrap and pound down and out form the center, until ¼ -inch (6 mm) thick and approximately 3 x 6 inches (8 x 15 cm) in size.

Place 1 slice of the salami and 1 slice of the cheese on half of a beef slice. Roll up then season the roll with salt and pepper only on one side. Repeat with the remaining beef slices.

Chop the remaining salami slices and set aside [I normally have about 1/2 cup.]

Heat 2 tablespoons of the olive oil and the butter in a large cast iron frying pan set on high heat until smoking, about 2-3 minutes.

Add the meat pockets and cook for 30 seconds on each side – you will need to do this in batches.

Transfer the browned meat to a plate and add the remaining oil to the same pan. Add the onion, garlic, red pepper flakes, the reserved chopped salami and the oregano and cook for 1 minute until the onion and garlic begin to brown.

Stir in the Marsala and boil until reduced by half, about 1 minute.

Add the diced tomatoes, beef bouillon, bay leaf and Italian seasoning and cook for 1-2 minutes until reduced and slightly thickened.

Return the browned meat to the pan and heat for 1-2 minutes. Adjust the seasonings as necessary.

Serve immediately.
